Rodger Matthew Dohm

Drawing is not only an art form, but the fundamental way to express your ideas, thoughts and feelings. Sketching is a language, and without it people are blind.

— Rodger Matthew Dohm

 

oway High's Engineering Academy Coordinator and 3D Computer Animation Instructor brings a wealth of knowledge in the areas of architecture, industrial design, drafting, engineering, manufacturing, computer science, robotics and computer animation to San Diego County. The computer and materials lab is outfitted with industrial standard software and hardware used professionally in industry across the nation. His renaissance and avant-garde approach, toward the field of computer graphics, 3D computer animation, game development, architecture, computer science and engineering has launched students into their field of choice. He is also the head coach of the Robotics Club on campus.

For the last 20 years, Rodger has been a leader in the nation for his model Engineering, 3D Computer Animation and Architectural Design curriculums. He has been nationally recognized through both the Ramona and Poway Unified School Districts. His curriculum follows and interest based renaissance approach for kids, which allow them to adopt their own interests and knowledge base into the field of entertainment and engineering design, commonly known as Project Based Learning (PBL). All courses meet UC/CSU college entrance requirements and are dual college or university credit. Many of Mr. Dohm's graduated students are currently working in the field of engineering, architecture and computer graphics/animation locally and abroad, while many others are in trade schools, or attending colleges and universities.  

Prior to teaching, Mr. Dohm double majored at Brigham Young University and received a Bachelor of Arts in the field of Math and Art Education. He also holds an MA in Educational Leadership from Point Loma Nazarene University. Mr. Dohm currently serves as a board member for the Ramona Unified School district and has been elected to three terms since 2006.
